When the novel Coronavirus - 19 (COVID)
emerged as a deadly force, we saw varied
responses from countries throughout the world.


Some took rapid, early, and decisive action
to confront COVID. Others, unfortunately,
took far too long to any take action,
much less action that had a serious impact.

In Europe, several countries were ravaged
by COVID, where hospitals had to turn away
patients due to the exceeding capacity
within weeks of the first patient in the country.

For the few countries that were able to
make the quick decisions to shut down
travel, promote social distancing and
reduce community spread.

These are called measures of containment
and mitigation. These are the best actions
we can take as a society to prevent spread
and save lives until a vaccine or similar
level of elimination or protection is available.
